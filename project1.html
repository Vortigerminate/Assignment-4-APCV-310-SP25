County Data Analysis

Project Overview

This project revolved around observing relational data within United States counties by comparing census data over the last six years.

Statistical Findings

  • Higher college completion tended to occur in areas of lower employment, but higher gross income.
  • Rural areas tended to have more drug abuse related deaths due to low proximity of treatment facilities.
  • Suicide more common in areas with significantly higher amounts of displaced youth.

Data Used

Census data gathered from County Health Rankings

National well-being, summarized.
